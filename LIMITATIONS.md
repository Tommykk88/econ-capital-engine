# Limitations and Model Risk

Every material simplification in this model, stated plainly. This is the
prepared answer to "what are the weaknesses of your model?"

## Data

1. **Catastrophe data.** The default catalog is **synthetic**, generated by
   `scripts/build_cat_catalog.py` and calibrated only to the order of
   magnitude of published storm/flood loss figures. Even with a real EM-DAT
   export (supported — drop `emdat.xlsx` into `data/raw/`), EM-DAT economic
   damage is coarse, survivor-biased, inconsistently reported across
   countries, and not insured loss. Production reinsurers use event-based
   vendor models (RMS/Moody's, Verisk) with explicit hazard, vulnerability
   and exposure modules — a fundamentally different architecture from
   fitting a GPD to historical aggregates.
2. **Inflation adjustment** of historical cat losses uses the source's own
   adjusted column (or none for the synthetic catalog); no exposure-growth
   normalization, which biases frequency-severity trends.
3. **Market data** covers ~2006 onward: it includes 2008 and 2020 but only
   one secular rate regime; the bond/equity correlation assumption embedded
   in the historical record may not hold forward.

## Credit

4. The portfolio is **synthetic** (rating buckets, lognormal exposures).
   Real models use internal ratings, PD term structures, sector/region
   multi-factor correlation, rating **migration** and spread risk, and
   stochastic LGD correlated with the cycle (downturn LGD). Here LGD is
   independent of the systematic factor, which understates tail losses.
5. One-factor dependence is the Basel IRB simplification; concentration in
   industries or countries is not captured.

## Market

6. The one-year horizon is built from 250-step GARCH paths with bootstrapped
   residuals — better than square-root-of-time, but residuals are drawn iid,
   so serial dependence beyond GARCH volatility clustering (e.g. momentum,
   regime persistence) is lost.
7. Constant portfolio weights for a year: no management action, no
   rebalancing drift, no liquidity horizon differentiation (FRTB's 10-250
   day liquidity horizons are ignored).
8. GARCH(1,1)-t is one model; no model averaging or EGARCH/GJR asymmetry
   (leverage effects are material for equity indices).

## Aggregation — the largest single source of model risk

9. **Copula dependence parameters are assumed, not estimated.** Annual risk
   type losses give far too few joint observations to estimate a copula;
   the correlation matrix is an economic judgment (credit-market 0.5,
   credit-cat 0.1, market-cat 0.2). This is precisely why the stress layer
   exists: the tornado chart quantifies how much capital moves when these
   assumptions are wrong.
10. The Gumbel copula used here is **exchangeable** (one theta for all
    pairs), so it cannot express asymmetric pairwise dependence; a vine
    copula would.
11. Marginals are mapped through **empirical** inverse CDFs of finite
    simulations, so the joint model cannot generate losses beyond each
    marginal's simulated maximum (tail truncation at roughly the
    1/200,000 quantile).

## Scope

12. No operational risk, no liquidity risk, no insurance reserve risk other
    than catastrophe, single currency, no taxes, no diversification across
    time, no management actions or contingent capital.
13. One-year aggregate horizon throughout; intra-year timing of cat events
    versus market drawdowns (and the funding strain when they coincide) is
    not modeled.
14. GPD threshold choice (90th percentile) is judgment supported by mean
    excess plots; a different defensible threshold moves the cat tail and
    therefore total capital — see the xi stress in the tornado.
